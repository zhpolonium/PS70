Final Project Brainstorming: As for my final project ideas, I’ve only come up with one so far. I wanted to build a (mini-version) prototype of a potential smart work desk. As the pandemic hits us hard in the past two years, it also creates a lasting change in the business landscapes and behaviors of work. More and more businesses are shifting to a work-from-home or at least a hybrid format. Most office workers don’t have designated work desks at home, so I think there is potential for a business venture. Some contextual secondary research: More studies have shown that about 20 to 25 percent of the workforce in advanced economies could work from home between three and five days a week without loss of productivity. This represents four to five times more remote work than before the pandemic and could prompt a large change in the geography of work, as individuals and companies shift out of large cities into suburbs and small cities (1). Office workers transitioning into working from home will likely start to get their own office equipment. Printers, photocopiers, and scanners are believed to be the bare essential business machines that every office worker cannot live without (2). The consumption of desktop printers, photocopiers, and scanners will likely increase in the next few years. <br> 1 https://www.forbes.com/sites/ashleystahl/2021/02/01/5-lasting-changes-to-expect-in-the-workplace-post-covid/?sh=4cd8903f213d <br> 2 https://www.homestratosphere.com/types-of-office-equipment/#C_Business_Machine To fill this product opportunity gap, a convenient, comfortable, and multi-functional smart work desk can be developed for office workers who work from home or executives who have private offices.
